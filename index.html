Stairways to Heaven

A Journey Through         Silverlake's Iconic Steps

One of LA's Best-Kept Secrets

Los Angeles is a city built on hills. In the 1920s, when most residents lacked personal cars, this posed an issue for people trying to get around. As more people began to live on hillsides, city planners built over 400 concrete staircases. These steps are scattered across LA's historic neighborhoods, from Mt. Washington to Santa Monica.

Over 50 of these staircases are concentrated in Silverlake, a neighborhood in Northeast Los Angeles. Silverlake's steps have long been a secret haven for young couples, stoners, and joggers. The stairs have undergone a revival in recent years, following the same trajectory as the neighborhood (which most would call gentrifiied). Local artists have painted designs and murals onto several of the staircases. The addition of this artwork has led the neighborhood's stairs to become an attraction for tourists and photographers.

This map displays only some of the many staircases that dot the Silverlake neighborhood. The stairs which I photographed are marked in green. I primarily journeyed along the Painted Staircases route which can be found here.

The Best Stairs in Silverlake

Hoover Walk

The Hoover Walk stairs are located at the end of Hoover Street. The mural, created by local muralist Ricardo Mendoza, is titled Fluid City Rising. It was dedicated in 2009. It has by far the most beautiful artwork of any of the sites I visited. Minus the litter, it's well worth the visit.

South Deloz/Hollyvista Stairs

This 3-level staircase begins at Prospect Ave and crosses Deloz and Hollyvista before finishing at Franklin Avenue. It is a great spot for anyone looking for an intense workout. It lacks artwork but makes up for it with a stunning West-facing view which looks directly down Hollywood Bouelvard.

Micheltorena Stairs

The Micheltorena Stairs have been called the most instagrammed staircase in LA, maybe the world. They are located directly off of Sunset. The artwork at the bottom level was created by Corrine Carrey. New paintings and sayings regularly appear on the multi-level staircase.

Howl/Animal Stairs

The Howl/Animal Stairs (name disputed) were painted by Joey Franks in 1994. The steps curve and have a thick tree cover, creating a dim, cool atmosphere that goes well with the animal paintings. The painting at the bottom, a strange mural that resembles ancient cave paintings, reads "Howled at the Moon, Felt Good."

Swan Steps

The Swan stairs have three levels and 287 steps which take you up to Swan Place and a great view of Downtown. They were painted by Evelyn Leigh.

Landa-Redesdale Stairs

This small staircase, which lacks an official name, provides the best view of the Silverlake Reservoir available to the public.

Piano Key Stairs

This staircase is tucked between two buildings and if you aren't careful, you might pass right by it (as I've apparently been doing my entire life). It is across the street from the Micheltorena Stairs on Sunset and was also painted by Corrine Carrey.
